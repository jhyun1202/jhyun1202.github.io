Hybrid End-Burning Rocket Engine

My Role: Igniter development, ignition system design

Developed Korea's second end-burning hybrid rocket engine with $62K funding from school to establish dedicated Rocket Combustion Laboratory. Designed and constructed a novel ignition system that enables controlled end-burning combustion. This research explores innovative propulsion techniques to improve rocket efficiency and thrust control characteristics.

The project involved extensive testing of ignition sequences, thermal protection systems, and combustion chamber design. Successfully demonstrated stable end-burning combustion with reproducible results across multiple test fires.

Solid Rocket Motor (KNO₃ Fueled)

My Role: Fuel development, documentation

Led the design and development of a solid rocket motor using potassium nitrate composite propellant. Successfully launched rocket to altitudes exceeding 400 meters. This project was part of BUZ Aerospace's effort to establish practical rocketry capabilities at the school level.

Project required securing $580K in funding to build the school's first comprehensive makerspace facility. Developed fuel formulation protocols, casting procedures, and safety systems. The rocket's successful flight demonstrated the viability of student-led aerospace projects and was featured in official school promotional materials.

Documented entire development process through photography and videography to create educational resources for future student engineers.

Acoustic Analysis of Fluid Motion in Non-Transparent Metallic Containers

Status: Ongoing Research

Developing acoustic-based sensor system to detect and analyze fuel sloshing behavior inside opaque metallic rocket tanks. This research addresses critical safety concerns in aerospace applications where visual monitoring is impossible during flight operations.

Built custom acoustic sensor arrays and signal processing algorithms to identify dangerous sloshing patterns that could destabilize rocket flight. Testing various tank geometries and fill levels to create comprehensive detection models.

Research has applications beyond rocketry, including commercial aviation fuel systems and industrial fluid transport.
